Role library — pre-assessed roles

A catalogue of roles already assessed and categorised. A match pre-fills a new worker’s assessment to save time and cite prior reasoning — it never determines status for an individual. Every worker still routes through a full per-worker assessment and, if borderline, the expert gate.

Stale entries (assessed before the current ruleset or a relevant case-law shift) are flagged and must be re-reviewed before they pre-fill against outdated logic.

Expert review queue

Borderline determinations land here. The hard gate: a borderline case cannot become an SDS without a qualified reviewer’s signed-off decision and reasoning — enforced in the data model, not just the screen.

SDS review calendar

A determination is not "set and forget". This schedules a re-assessment of every SDS so the workforce stays compliant — the periodic-review element of reasonable care.

Review rule

Each determination is re-assessed every 12 months, or immediately when the role, rate or working practices materially change. A role change auto-triggers a fresh SDS and resets the clock.

Audit trail & disputes

Every determination, routing and sign-off is logged with a timestamp and owner — the evidence of "reasonable care" HMRC asks for first in an enquiry.
