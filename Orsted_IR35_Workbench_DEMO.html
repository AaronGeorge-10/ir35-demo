New status assessment

A status questionnaire grouped around the three key employment-status tests (substitution, control, mutuality), each with several questions, plus supporting minor tests. The key tests gate the outcome — strong minor factors don't rescue a determination when the key tests fail. Demonstrates the hybrid model: instant result for clear cases, automatic routing to a human expert for borderline ones.

Assignment questionnaire

Completed by:
Illustrative weighting only. A production tool would align to current case law and HMRC guidance, with borderline outcomes reviewed by a qualified IR35 specialist before any SDS is issued. In a live build the client and contractor each complete their own version of this questionnaire and the tool cross-references the two, flagging any divergence for review. Each answer also takes a free-text note so the evidence behind it is captured with the determination.

How the result is reached

Each of the three key tests now has several questions; they are weighted heavily and gate the outcome. The minor tests refine it but can't override failing key tests. One hard override applies.

1. Personal service & substitutionKEY 3 questions — substitute right, basis of refusal, actual use.
2. ControlKEY 3 questions — method, hours/location, supervision.
3. Mutuality of obligationKEY 3 questions — offer, accept, pay in downtime.
Minor testsMINOR 6 questions — risk, payment, equipment, integration, exclusivity, business-on-own-account.
Office-holder?OVERRIDE A director / office-holder is automatically inside.

SDS review calendar

A determination is not "set and forget". This schedules a re-assessment of every SDS so the workforce stays compliant over time — and evidences the periodic-review element of reasonable care.

Review rule

Each determination is re-assessed every 12 months, or immediately when the role, contract terms or working practices materially change. A role change auto-triggers a fresh SDS and resets the clock.

Audit trail & disputes

Every determination, change and challenge is logged with a timestamp and owner — the evidence of "reasonable care" HMRC asks for first in an enquiry.
